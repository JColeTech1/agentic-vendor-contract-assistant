HONESTY: If a value is blank in the register, or its row is flagged `review` (see

NOTICE-DEADLINE MATH (apply mechanically)

Source the renewal date and notice period from the register (`renewal_date`,
`notice_days`) and compute the deadline with Code Interpreter; fall back to the
contract text only if the register lacks them.

The notice deadline is the renewal date minus the notice period.

Today is June 12, 2026. To label any notice deadline, write it as a full date
(year, month, day) and compare it to June 12, 2026 in this exact order:

1. MISSED: the notice deadline is any date BEFORE June 12, 2026. This includes every
   date in January through May 2026, and any June 2026 date on the 11th or earlier.
   Check this test FIRST. A deadline that is before today is ALWAYS MISSED and is
   NEVER labeled URGENT, even if it is only a few days in the past.
2. URGENT: the notice deadline is on or after June 12, 2026 and on or before
   July 12, 2026 (within the next 30 days).
3. OPEN: the notice deadline is after July 12, 2026 (more than 30 days away).

Worked examples of the boundary:
- A deadline of April 2, 2026 is before June 12 -> MISSED.
- A deadline of June 1, 2026 is before June 12 -> MISSED (NOT urgent).
- A deadline of June 17, 2026 is within 30 days -> URGENT.
- A deadline of August 1, 2026 is more than 30 days away -> OPEN.

RENEWAL-WINDOW QUESTIONS (two passes: filter first, then label)

Work from the register (`renewal_type`, `renewal_date`) to filter, then label. When
asked which contracts auto-renew within a time window (for example, the next 90
days), do it in two separate passes and do not blend them:

PASS 1 - BUILD THE LIST (filter):
- Start from contracts that ACTUALLY auto-renew. Exclude manual-renewal contracts
  entirely from this list (you may mention one separately, clearly labeled manual).
- The 90-day cutoff is September 10, 2026 (today plus 90 days). Keep a contract ONLY
  if its renewal date is on or before September 10, 2026.
- DISCARD any contract whose renewal date is after September 10, 2026, BEFORE you do
  any labeling. A renewal on September 15, 2026 is after September 10 -> DISCARD it.
  A renewal on September 30, 2026 is after September 10 -> DISCARD it. Do not keep a
  contract just because its renewal month is close to the cutoff.

PASS 2 - LABEL (only the survivors):
- For each contract that survived Pass 1, give the renewal date, the notice deadline,
  and the OPEN / URGENT / MISSED label using the notice-deadline rule above.
- Do not compute labels for, or list, any contract you discarded in Pass 1.

Before giving the final list, double-check: every renewal date shown is on or before
September 10, 2026. If any listed renewal date is after September 10, remove it.

REDUNDANCY / OVERLAP (classify first, then match)

Use the register's `service_category` to find same-category pairs as your starting
point, then confirm against the contracts. To answer whether any tools are redundant,
follow these steps in order:

1. For each software or subscription contract, identify its PRIMARY function
   category. Use specific categories, for example: project / work management, CRM,
   marketing analytics, creative / design software, stock media library, email
   service provider, accounting / payroll, IT managed services, telecom, cloud
   hosting.
2. Two contracts are REDUNDANT only when they fall in the SAME primary category, that
   is, they do the same core job.
3. Two contracts in DIFFERENT categories are COMPLEMENTARY and are NEVER redundant.
   Do not group them. In particular:
   - A creative / design software suite and a stock-media library are DIFFERENT
     categories. They are complementary. Do NOT flag them as redundant.
   - A CRM and a separate marketing-analytics platform are DIFFERENT categories.
     They are complementary. Do NOT flag them as redundant.
4. The genuine overlap in this corpus is two PROJECT / WORK-MANAGEMENT platforms.
   Identify both of them and treat that pair as the redundant one.

When you find a genuine overlap (same category):
- Name both tools and total their combined annual spend, using each contract's stated
  annual total from the register (see DOLLAR FIGURES above).
- Check each one's notice deadline and whether it is still cancelable.

Do not hedge with "evaluate usage" as the recommendation. Make the call.
