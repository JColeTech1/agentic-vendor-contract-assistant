# Employee Benefits Brokerage and Administration Agreement

**Contract ID:** KBG-BEN-2024-0901
**Broker:** Keystone Benefits Group, LLC ("Broker")
**Client:** Contoso Marketing, LLC ("Client")
**Effective Date:** September 1, 2024

---

## 1. Services

1.1 Broker shall act as Client's broker of record for group health, dental, vision, life, and disability benefits, and shall provide benefits administration, open-enrollment support, and compliance guidance for approximately **150 employees**.

## 2. Term and Renewal

2.1 **Initial Term.** Two (2) years from the Effective Date, ending **August 31, 2026**.

2.2 **Automatic Renewal.** This Agreement **renews automatically** for successive one-year terms unless terminated. Renewal date: **September 1, 2026**.

2.3 **Notice.** Either party may terminate or elect non-renewal by written notice **at least sixty (60) days** before the renewal date (by **July 3, 2026**).

## 3. Compensation

3.1 Broker is compensated through a combination of carrier-paid commissions and a flat annual administration fee of **USD $24,000**, invoiced quarterly, **net 30**.

3.2 **No fixed escalation clause** applies; the administration fee is held flat for each term and any change is negotiated at renewal. Carrier commissions are set by the carriers.

## 4. Data Protection

4.1 Broker handles employee **personal data and protected health information (PHI)**. Broker acts as Client's **Business Associate** and the parties have executed a **HIPAA Business Associate Agreement (Exhibit B)** and a general **Data Processing Addendum**, both forming part of this Agreement.

4.2 Broker shall safeguard PHI/PII with administrative, physical, and technical safeguards; limit use to permitted purposes; and report any breach of unsecured PHI within the timeframes required by HIPAA and applicable state law.

## 5. Confidentiality

Each party shall maintain the confidentiality of the other's non-public information and of employee data indefinitely with respect to PHI.

## 6. Liability

Broker's liability is capped at the greater of the fees paid in the prior twelve months or $100,000, except for breaches of Section 4 and willful misconduct.

## 7. Governing Law

Governed by the laws of the State of Washington.
